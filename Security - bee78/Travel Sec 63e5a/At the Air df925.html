At the Airport

Preparing for your flight

  • If you must stopover, do it in a major city
    • for example, on your way to San Francisco from Europe, stop in New York, not Dallas.
    • you will be processed through customs upon your first stop in the country
    • smaller cities' customs officers may be less open-minded and more scrutinizing about foreigners visiting
  • Always check-in online, before your flight
  • Prepare for your travel by adding an identifying mark of some sort on your work laptop, for example, an unassuming, nonpolitical sticker. (Any sticker you can find in a dollar store or in a school section of any store will work, such as a sticker of a fruit or a pencil, so long as the placement of it on the computer when closed will enable you to differentiate between your computer and someone else's at a glance.)
  • If you have legal insurance, use the member card issued to you by the legal firm which describes your name, membership number, legal firm ("Lawyer and Lawyer, LLP"), and legal firm's phone number by taping it over completely with transparent "scotch" tape, card facing up in the (vacant metal spot at) the left of your mousepad/trackpad. If airport security opens the laptop (this is an attempt to turn it on and see what's there), they will see the "legal protection" card before they can attempt to turn the laptop on. On one occasion having this card in place as described above has resulted in airport security quickly halting their attempt to open a work laptop of an agent and waving the agent through the checkpoint.
  • dropping off baggage - always keep your work devices with you (when passing through security, keep the items at or beneath your hand or arm, or if this is not possible because you are going through a scanner / checkpoint and / or are required to pass a laptop through a scanner, keep a line of sight to where the computer is going at all times).

Flying under the radar

  • The best way to avoid an escalation of problems is to seem like any other uninteresting random person – blend.
  • don't wear your Bitcoin shirt, Silk Road shirt, anti-government paraphernalia
  • don't forget to take water bottles out of your bag, which could prompt an invasive search of everything
  • don't make a political statement by hassling the security agents
  • don't comment on the person checking you in that all this KYC to get on a plane is bullshit
  • don't expose your line of work to stranger(s) on the plane. You're on your way to a new place where you don't know the rules. They might be innocent but they could mention you to somebody who isn't. People rob banks for less than you might have in your bitcoin wallet.

Remember the goal:  get to your destination with minimal friction. We're not going to change the world with Bitcoin if we keep having our devices confiscated and we're denied travel because we're obsessed with short-term injustices.

Prepare for invasive device tampering

  • Officials might demand that you unlock your laptop or phone for them
    • Never do this no matter what
    • If you want to be super secure, keep your OS encrypted on a USB drive and leave the machine as a shell
    • it will also help to fully discharge your battery and power down so that you have a softer excuse for not logging in for them (note: it is advisable to at least leave a trickle of a charge on the computer so if worst comes to worst and the computer is turned on by security, it cannot be claimed that you were purposefully circumventing by having zero power – this claim is sometimes made, so a "two to five percent" charge may be better than a zero percent charge prior to entering an airport - use your best judgment)

If your devices are taken out of your sight, assume compromised, shred and buy replacements

Prepare for invasive questioning

  • It is almost always better to say you are traveling on tourism rather than say anything about crypto
  • Never mention that you are going to do work at your destination because this could trigger work visa issues

On the airplane

  • Under no circumstance use public USB ports to charge your devices.
  • We don't recommend to use in-air wifi. If you do, you are obliged to use a VPN
  • Keep your laptop with you at all times
